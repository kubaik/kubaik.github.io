## The Problem Most Developers Miss
Developers often focus on writing clean, efficient code, but neglect the business side of their job. They spend countless hours optimizing algorithms, reducing memory usage, and improving performance, but fail to understand how their work contributes to the company's bottom line. This lack of understanding can hinder their career advancement, as they are not able to effectively communicate the value of their work to management. For example, a developer may spend weeks optimizing a database query to reduce execution time by 30%, but if they cannot explain how this improvement will increase revenue or reduce costs, their efforts may go unrecognized. To get promoted faster in tech, developers need to understand how their work fits into the larger business strategy and be able to communicate their contributions in a way that resonates with management.
