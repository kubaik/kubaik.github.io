AI Content Policy

How we use AI tools, what human review covers, and what we guarantee.

Our Commitment in One Paragraph

Every article on Kubai Kevin is written on a topic selected by the author (Kubai Kevin) from his own production experience, then drafted end-to-end by LLMs through an automated pipeline. Given the publishing volume, articles are not individually fact-checked or hand-edited by a human before going live. Automated quality gates (below) catch thin, duplicate, and boilerplate content before publication, and the author reviews and corrects specific articles when readers flag issues.

1. How AI is Used on This Site

Kubai Kevin uses large language model (LLM) APIs — including models from Mistral, Google Gemini, Meta Llama (via Groq and other providers), and similar services — to:

  • Draft article structure and outlines based on an author-selected topic.
  • Generate prose that is then reviewed and edited.
  • Suggest SEO keywords, meta descriptions, and social media copy.
  • Automate repetitive formatting tasks (headings, code blocks, tables).

AI tools are used as a writing assistant, not as the final publisher. The author remains responsible for all published content.

2. What AI Does NOT Do on This Site

Not scraped or aggregated

We do not scrape, copy, or rephrase content from other websites. Every article starts from a topic brief, not from existing web content.

Not published without review

Our pipeline includes automated quality gates: minimum word count (1,500+), boilerplate detection, and content quality validation before any article is saved.

Not keyword-stuffed

Content prompts explicitly ban keyword stuffing, filler phrases, and AI-detectable clichés. Quality validators check for these automatically.

Illustrative, not audited, figures

Specific numbers, benchmarks, and cost figures in articles are generated as realistic illustrations of the pattern being discussed, not pulled from a verified source per article. Every post says so explicitly in its "About this article" footer — treat figures as directional and confirm them against current official documentation before relying on them in production.

3. Quality Controls in Our Publishing Pipeline

Before an article is saved and published, it passes through automated checks that enforce:

  • Minimum 1,500 words — thin content is rejected outright.
  • Boilerplate detection — template artifacts or placeholder text trigger automatic discard.
  • Filler phrase detection — AI-pattern phrases ("dive into", "game-changer", "it's important to note") are flagged and the article is regenerated.
  • E-E-A-T signal injection — every article includes an author byline, publication date, last-reviewed date, and an editorial standards footer.
  • Duplicate title detection — Jaccard similarity is checked against all existing posts to prevent near-duplicate articles.
  • Stale year scrubbing — dates and statistics are validated to use current year references.

Articles that fail these gates are discarded. No fallback or placeholder article is published in their place.

4. Author's Role

Kubai Kevin is the author and editor of all content on this site. His role in the pipeline includes:

  • Defining the topic list from personal production experience (not keyword research alone).
  • Maintaining the automated quality gate code that decides what is and isn't published (minimum length, duplicate detection, boilerplate and filler-phrase rejection).
  • Reviewing and correcting individual articles when readers report an error via the contact page.
  • Periodically spot-checking the published post library for accuracy and policy compliance.

The author's LinkedIn profile and contact details are published on the About page for full transparency.

5. Corrections Policy

If an article contains a factual error, an outdated tool version, or inaccurate code, please contact us. We will investigate, correct the article on a best-effort basis, and note the correction date on the affected post.

6. AI Disclosure Compliance

This page serves as the AI content disclosure for Kubai Kevin in jurisdictions that require or recommend disclosure of AI-assisted content generation. We believe transparency is both ethically correct and practically important for reader trust.

We monitor evolving regulatory requirements around AI content labelling (including EU AI Act guidance, FTC guidance, and platform-specific requirements) and will update this policy accordingly.
